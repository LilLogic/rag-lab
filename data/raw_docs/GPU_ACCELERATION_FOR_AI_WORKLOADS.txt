Graphics Processing Units were originally designed for parallel graphical computations but have become central to modern AI infrastructure. Their massively parallel architecture allows efficient execution of matrix operations required by deep learning workloads. Compared to CPUs, GPUs can process thousands of mathematical operations simultaneously.

Deep learning frameworks such as PyTorch and TensorFlow rely heavily on GPU acceleration for training neural networks. Operations involving tensor multiplication, gradient computation, and attention mechanisms benefit enormously from GPU parallelism. Training state-of-the-art models on CPUs alone would often be computationally impractical.

Modern AI infrastructure increasingly uses clusters of GPUs connected through high-bandwidth interconnects such as NVLink or InfiniBand. These architectures enable distributed model training across multiple machines while minimizing communication bottlenecks between devices.

GPU resource management has become a major engineering discipline in AI systems. Organizations optimize GPU scheduling, batching strategies, memory utilization, and inference throughput in order to maximize hardware efficiency and reduce operational costs.
